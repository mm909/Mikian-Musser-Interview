My name is Mikian Musser and I am more than a resume. I love working with code, I enjoy working with kids, and I couldn’t imagine doing anything else.

I chose to study CS because I wanted to make video games. I wanted to construct worlds with rich atmospheres, compelling characters, and meaningful storylines. It was all about taking my ideas out of the notebook and expressing them on screen.

While videogames were the inspiration, the coding experience is what kept me in this field. I love the process of breaking down a problem, writing the functions, testing, and debugging. I love the late nights that turn into early mornings. I love collaborating with my peers and the friendly competition that comes with it. I enjoy the academic process of writing notes, completing assignments, and taking exams. Lately my interests have taken me away from game design and into neural networks and artificial intelligence.

Five years ago, I started volunteering with Sunshine Nevada who hosts summer camps for kids with disabilities. At camp Mend-A-Heart I met kids who were very sick but always had bright smiles and loud laughs. It was here that I found a passion for working with kids. There isn’t a better feeling in the world than having a kid look up to you and want to follow your example. I enjoy having kids in the classroom because I have the opportunity to teach them and be a positive role model in their life. I love challenging myself to present information simple enough for kids to learn. I am reminded of the famous Google interview question, “Explain a database in three sentences to your eight-year-old nephew.”

I couldn’t imagine doing anything else. Almost inexplicably, there is something about working with kids that just makes me so happy - so grateful. While working for iD Tech I would stay up late working on lesson plans and creating example projects. I would come in early to clean up the classroom and markup the whiteboard. Despite the long and sometimes unpaid hours, I was smiling Monday morning through Friday afternoon. I was excited to come to work everyday. Coding is my passion, teaching is my purpose. Thank you for your consideration.

Teaching — iD Tech, Rice University

I taught visual programming, Lego Mindstorms, and Scratch to classes of eight kids ages seven to ten years old. I created lesson plans, homework assignments, and coding challenges for kids with variable math and coding skill levels. I balanced teaching the class and working with each student one on one to ensure each student was learning and felt included.

Sunshine Nevada — Counselor

For the last five years I have volunteered as a counselor for Sunshine Nevada who hosts several summer camps for kids with disabilities. Camp TLC and IAM are for kids on the autism spectrum and camp Mend-A-Heart is for kids with congenital heart defects. Each camp I work with two to four kids and lead them through typical summer camp activities. I have ample experience working with kids on the autism spectrum and feel comfortable working with them in a classroom setting.
